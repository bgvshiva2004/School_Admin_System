CBSE

  • CBSE is a national-level board of education in India, functioning under the Union Government of India. It caters to schools across the country and several international schools.
  • One of the significant roles of CBSE is to prescribe and update the curriculum for affiliated schools. The board ensures a standardized and comprehensive curriculum to promote uniformity in education at the secondary and senior secondary levels.
  • CBSE conducts the All India Secondary School Examination (AISSE) for Class 10 and the All India Senior School Certificate Examination (AISSCE) for Class 12.

Cambridge Assessment International Education Curriculum

  • The Cambridge International curriculum sets a global standard for education, and is recognised by universities and employers worldwide. CI curriculum is flexible, challenging and inspiring, culturally sensitive yet international in approach.
  • Cambridge IGCSE assessment takes place at the end of the course (every year during March) and can include written, oral, coursework and practical assessment. This broadens opportunities for students to demonstrate their learning, particularly when their first language is not English. Grades are benchmarked using eight internationally recognised grades. (A*, A, B, C, D, E, F and G).
  • The list of subjects are : English, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Economics, Business, Accounts, History, Psychology, Information Technology, Mathematics.

Pedagogies

  • Pedagogy involves the study and application of various teaching methods and strategies.
  • Effective pedagogy takes into account the diverse learning styles and preferences of students.
  • In modern education, pedagogy often involves the integration of technology to enhance learning experiences. This may include the use of multimedia, online resources, and interactive tools to support teaching and engage students.
